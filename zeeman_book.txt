4.4

The role of collisions in resonance polarization

Collisions with surrounding particles can be classified into two types with respect
to their influence on spectral lines and their polarization properties.
a) Exciting collisions
These collisions, generally produced by electrons, are capable of exciting
the atom to its upper level. If the distribution of velocities of the colliders is
isotropical, these collisions give rise to unpolarized radiation in the de-excitation
mechanism of the atom (unless a magnetic field is present, capable of introducing
an appreciable Zeeman splitting). If, on the contrary, the velocity distribution
of the colliders is anisotropical, these collisions are capable of introducing atomic
polarization and, as a consequence, polarized radiation in the de-excitation pro¬
cess (Impact polarization). A phenomenon analogue to the Hanle effect is also
present (Hanle effect for impact polarization).

The equation derived in the former section can be solved with various degrees of
sophistication to give the values of the emerging Stokes parameters as a function

comparison of observations with the results obtained through such solutions;
it is then important to be aware of the various approximations that underlie
the different methods of solution (and the radiative transfer equation itself) in
order to give reliable assesments on solar magnetic fields. In the following we
will give a presentation of the various methods of solution in increasing order of
complexity.
